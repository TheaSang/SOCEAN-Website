Social Ocean Energy

A Social Learning Approach to
Global Marine Renewable Infrastructures

Marine Renewable Energy (MRE) technologies are being heavily promoted on European, Asian and American seaboards. These new ocean infrastructures are set to become a key part of sustainable energy transformations. Yet decades of experience with solar and wind parks have shown that green energy technologies are often beset with social and environmental justice concerns.

We therefore need to study the impact of marine renewables being established on global coasts with a methodology that actively brings academic research in to support resilient, socially-acceptable MRE design and deployment. Recasting the goal of research as social learning, this project purposefully bridges the divide between 'basic' and 'applied' research.

Social Ocean Energy (SOCEAN) takes a social learning approach in pioneering the first ethnographic study of marine renewable energy between coastal development hotspots in Asia and North America.
